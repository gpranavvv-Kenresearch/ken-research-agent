Vietnam Sugar Market Climbs to USD 2.63 Billion by 2030 at 4.0% CAGR: Ken Research

Vietnam just milled a record 1.266 million tonnes of sugar, up 14.2% year on year at an ASEAN-leading 6.69 tonnes per hectare, yet its mills are drowning in their own success, with inventories now above 70% of output. That paradox defines a market valued at USD 2,080 million in 2024 and projected to reach USD 2,632 million by 2030 at a 4.0% CAGR, a climb the Vietnam Sugar Market Report maps across application, channel and competitive segments, up from a 2.6% historical pace over 2019 to 2024. In volume terms, consumption rises from 1.90 million metric tons in 2024 to 2.28 million metric tons by 2030.

This analysis draws on data from Ken Research market modelling, Vietnam Sugarcane and Sugar Association (VSSA) disclosures, Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development data, and independent sugar-sector benchmarking.

  • By trade, official sugar imports fell 18.9% year on year in the first half of 2024 as trade-defense duties tightened, even with import dependency near 54% of demand.

Why Is Demand Trailing a Record 1.27 Million Tonne Harvest?

The squeeze is on the demand side, not supply. The 2024 to 2025 crop produced a record 1.266 million tonnes at an ASEAN-best 6.69 tonnes per hectare, yet inventories have climbed above 70% of total output, a record high, as beverage makers shift toward high-fructose corn syrup. Domestic prices still firmed 12.8% year on year to VND 21,200 per kg in the first half of 2024, but that strength rests on import control, not consumption growth.

Inside the 40% Food and Beverage Engine of Vietnam Sugar Demand

Industrial buyers, not households, set the trajectory. Food and beverage processing absorbed USD 832 million, or 40.0%, of 2024 demand, lifted by accommodation and food-service value added rising 9.76% and international arrivals of 17.6 million visitors. Household consumption, by contrast, grows at just 1.9%, confirming that the next USD 552 million of value is an industrial story.

Imports, Smuggling and the 54% Dependency Gap Reshaping Supply

Even with record milling, Vietnam still imports near 54% of demand, and the supply chain is being reset by policy. Official imports fell 18.9% year on year in the first half of 2024 as anti-dumping duties bit, with the import quota set at 121,000 of 126,000 tonnes (Vietnam Ministry of Industry and Trade market bulletin). Smuggling persists, with unofficial inflows once cited at 756,000 tonnes, keeping pressure on domestic mills. What Mills, Investors and Buyers Must Do Before the 2025 to 2030 Window Narrows

With inventories above 70% and the market expanding only 4.0% a year, the advantage goes to players who fix demand, not just output.

  • Mills: Shift mix toward liquid and specialty formats growing at 6.8%, and monetize bagasse-to-power to offset thin refined-sugar margins.
  • Investors: Weight toward the consolidated leaders, where TTC Sugar's 46% share signals pricing power as 25 mills rationalize.
  • Industrial buyers: Lock multi-year supply now while trade-defense duties hold imports down 18.9% and domestic prices stay near VND 21,200 per kg.
