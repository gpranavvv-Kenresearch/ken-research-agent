Why Does Nomex Honeycomb Hold 68% of Saudi Cabin Flooring?

The answer is fire safety and weight. Nomex aramid cores command roughly 68% of 2024 demand because their flame retardancy and stiffness-to-weight ratio suit commercial cabins, while aluminum honeycomb holds 24% in wide-body and cargo roles. For manufacturers sizing the upstream core supply behind that mix, the input economics are detailed in the Saudi Arabia Honeycomb Core Materials Market, where core availability directly caps local panel output. Hybrid thermoplastic composites, still only 8% of demand, are the fastest-emerging cut as business jets prioritize acoustic and thermal insulation.

Can Vision 2030 Localize a USD 85 Million Import-Led Market?

The intent is funded, but the base is thin. Vision 2030 issued 83 industrial licenses worth SR 950 million, around USD 253 million, opened 58 factories with SR 2.85 billion of investment, and set a 50% local-content floor for military aerospace (Vision 2030 National Industrial Development and Logistics Program). Yet most ballistic-grade and aerospace composite inputs are still imported, a gap that the broader Middle East Composites Market shows is closing only slowly across the region. Adjacent localization in springs, fasteners and structural parts is tracked in the Saudi Arabia Aerospace Defense C Class Parts Market, where the same supplier-base constraint applies.
